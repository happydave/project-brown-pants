## Architectural Boundaries

Required: `/crates/sim/` (`sounding_sim`) depends only on Bevy sub-crates (`bevy_app`, `bevy_ecs`, `bevy_time`, `bevy_diagnostic`, `bevy_log`), never the `bevy` umbrella — so it is rendering-free and runs headless.
Forbidden: `/crates/sim/` pulling in rendering or windowing. Verify: `cargo tree -p sounding_sim` shows no `bevy_render`, `bevy_winit`, or `wgpu`.
Required: every change to simulation state is `Command`-driven through the executor — no input system, autopilot, or bus client mutates state directly; they emit `Command` messages. Two arms apply them: **field mutations** (warp, pause, maneuver) go through the pure `apply_command` in `/crates/sim/src/command.rs`; **structural changes** (component insert/remove, spawn/despawn — e.g. the gear swap on `Command::SetGear`, WI 508) go through a dedicated `Command`-triggered system, since they are outside `apply_command`'s `(clock, orbit)` reach. The invariant is the routing (a `Command` drives it), not the single function.
Required: external processes (`/crates/companion/`, future second screen / MCP AI) act on the simulation only via the runtime bus (`GET /telemetry`, `POST /command`). The bus injects `Command`s; it never mutates state.
Required: the Bevy Remote Protocol is compiled only under the app's `dev` feature, absent from default/release builds, and is distinct from the runtime bus.

returns `None` without wheels (the rover-vs-rocket discriminator) and builds the `powertrain` (WI 609) from the build's devices/parts. `set_steer` applies coordinated counter-steer (angle ∝ each wheel's longitudinal CoM offset, WI 616). **Impact damage + collision feel (WI 618):** `shear_on_impact(closing_speed, dir)` shears the wheels facing a hit when their closing speed × side-share exceeds a material-rated `shear_speed` (an inert wheel exerts/drives nothing) — keyed to *speed* not sustained force, so leaning never shears; **belly skids** (`belly_points`, one per wheel corner) let a wheel-less/bottomed-out chassis rest on the ground instead of tunnelling; `wheel_inertia` is the **physical** `½·m·r²` (from the real wheel-part mass) so a stopped rover's wheels don't catapult it; and `apply_brake` clamps the brake at zero spin (stops a wheel, never reverses it) so braking locks cleanly at the grip limit instead of chattering. **Drivetrain components + fall damage + scale-aware feel (WI 630):** `assemble_rover` now composes each wheel from **separate suspension/rim/tire components** (a `Part.station` group, or a legacy monolithic wheel migrated via `WheelPart::to_components`) through one `compose_wheel` — effective radius = rim + tire profile, **per-wheel tire grip/slip** (the tire scales surface μ and the slip curve, so a tire is *felt*), and the contact normal spring is the **tire spring in series with the suspension** (so a rigid/absent strut rides on tire compliance). The **angular damping is now an inertia-scaled rate** (×mean moment of inertia) rather than an absolute coefficient — the old constant pinned light editor-scale builds upright (a "magical force") while being negligible on heavy ones; the rate is consistent across sizes (light builds roll, heavy builds keep their tuning). **Fall damage:** `shear_on_landing(closing_speed)` shears wheels uniformly on a hard vertical drop (the app tracks airborne downward speed via `airborne(&terrain)` and applies it on touchdown). **Unsprung-mass quarter-car (WI 631a):** each assembled wheel gains its own **vertical DOF + (capped) unsprung mass** (`Wheel::axle_drop`/`axle_drop_vel`): a two-mass corner — suspension spring-damper between chassis and axle, **tire spring** between axle and ground — replacing the WI 630 single series spring (which survives only as the analytic **massless limit**, `unsprung_mass → 0`, for hand-built fixtures). The tire force is the **dynamic ground load** that scales grip (load transfer falls out); the chassis feels only the suspension reaction. The axle's 1-D DOF is integrated **implicitly** (backward-Euler scalar solve, denominator `1 + (dt/m)(C+K·dt) > 1` ⇒ unconditionally stable). A **compliant** (sprung) wheel runs this quarter-car (its own unsprung-mass hop). A **no-suspension** wheel is the model's **rigid limit** (`k_susp → ∞`): the axle is *locked* to the chassis and the body rides **directly on the tyre** — the same tire-on-body contact used for massless fixtures (a rigid connection is a lock, not a stiff spring; modelling it as a stiff spring made the chassis coupling lively — spawn bounce, mid-air "grab", inverted slide). A no-suspension wheel keeps its mass on the body, is **critically damped** for its carried load (no spawn bounce), and exerts zero force airborne (no graze grab, no inverted slide — it rests on the belly contact and stops). A **zero-travel suspension** is still compliant (a soft strut, no droop), so it behaves like suspension, not like no-suspension. Any **mix** of compliant and no-suspension wheels on one vehicle is fine. `assemble_rover` **reclassifies** the unsprung mass off the sprung body's translational mass (weight conserved; CoM/inertia kept **full** so corner-mounted wheels still resist body rotation — stripping their inertia tumbled heavy-wheel builds). Coefficients are **scale-relative**: the axle-spring ceiling and the static-load grip cap (`GRIP_LOAD_FACTOR`, tire load-sensitivity — so bump-load spikes don't multiply into tumbles) are mass-relative; the axle has droop+bump travel stops. The WI 611 angular drag acts **only under substantial ground contact** (`>0.1·weight` or a belly skid) so a tumble keeps spinning mid-air — even a low-hanging wheel grazing the ground just off the ramp won't damp it (angular momentum is conserved airborne); a wheel's **tangential forces fade once the rover is past upright** (an upside-down wheel lays down no traction, so a tumbled rover can't pump itself up — the normal support never fades, so it can't tunnel; past upright the faded slip/drive forces are replaced by a **dissipative Coulomb friction** opposing the contact slip, so an inverted rover **slides to a stop** instead of coasting on its frictionless wheel-springs forever — WI 631b playtest); and a no-suspension tyre is **critically damped for its carried load** (the body rides directly on it) so it neither hops nor bounces on spawn. Headless, validated by no-launch/no-tumble/no-spin + drives-forward-without-wheelie + obstacle-contact + counter-steer + rollover + shear/belly/braking + migration-equivalence + felt-tire (grip/compliance) + light-build drivable-and-rollable + ramp-launch + landing-shear + quarter-car (mass-conservation/rest-load/wheel-hop/hop-settle/scale-stability) + many-axle (6/8/18-wheel) + no-suspension/mixed/zero-travel tests. **Component failure modes (WI 631b):** below the catastrophic mount shear, an impact (or hard landing) now fails a wheel's components in a **graded ladder** — **tire blowout** (`blow_tire`: grip collapses to a residual, the wheel drops to its **`rim_radius`** and runs on the rim, more rolling drag), **rim bend** (`bend_rim`: extra rolling resistance + a steer/camber `steer_bias`), then **damper failure** (`blow_damper`: that corner's damping is cut → bouncy) — and only *above* the shear speed does the wheel tear off (WI 618 unchanged). Ratings are fractions of the per-wheel shear speed in an **upper band approaching shear** (≈`0.70 / 0.82 / 0.92` for tire/rim/damper, tuned in playtest so only a genuinely hard hit damages a part — a gentle bump does nothing), so the ordering `tire_burst < rim_bend < damper_fail < shear` is automatic and scale-relative; effects are **latched** and **monotone-softening** (grip/damping/radius down, resistance up) so a failure can never wake the kraken; `ImpactOutcome` reports which wheels blew a tyre / bent a rim / blew a damper (via the shared `apply_impact`, called by both `shear_on_impact` and `shear_on_landing`). Failures ride the **existing impact + hard-landing seam** — an in-step over-deflection/bottoming trigger was tried and **dropped** (the stability-capped tyre makes a heavy corner's normal static deflection indistinguishable from an overload, and legitimate hard off-road bottoming would blow every damper). Validated by graded-ladder / latching-no-compounding / run-on-rim / bent-rim+blown-damper / damaged-rover-stays-bounded tests. **Ride pass (WI 631b playtest):** the tire spring is **floored at `TIRE_TO_SUSPENSION_RATIO`× the (mass-sized) suspension rate** so the real-world ordering — tire stiffer than the spring — holds at any build scale; without it a heavy rover's auto-sized suspension can exceed an authored (absolute) tire rate, so the body+wheel bob together on the soft tire (a mode the suspension damper can't reach) and **wallow under-damped after a landing**. Flooring restores suspension-borne, well-damped heave (a softer authored tire still rides a little lower within the physical range; below the floor tires clamp alike). Validated by `assembled_tire_is_stiffer_than_the_suspension` + the felt-tire ride-height test. **Hull–terrain contact + geometric wheels (WI 634):** the chassis now collides with the terrain at a **bounded, orientation-general set of hull-shell points** (`hull_points`, exposed-face centres over the voxel shell, capped + subsampled — generalising the WI 618 belly skids), each a mass-relative penalty (`HULL_CONTACT_OMEGA`, critically damped) against the analytic `Terrain`, with a **per-point force cap** (`HULL_MAX_G`) so a deep/false penetration at a terrain discontinuity can never explode the body. So the chassis no longer ghosts through terrain between wheels, and a wheel-less / inverted rover **rests on its hull** (works at any attitude — the points face every direction). Wheel forces are now **purely geometric** (the WI 631a/631b past-upright force fade was *removed*): a wheel works whenever it actually touches the ground, any orientation (a flipped-but-wheels-down rover still drives), and an upside-down rover's wheels point up, don't reach the ground, and so exert nothing — the hull catches it (no phantom-wheel bounce, no orientation special-case). `hull_penetration` is a debug signal; the hull-contact flag feeds the WI 611 angular-drag gate. Validated by hull-points-dense/oriented/bounded, nominal-inert, wheel-less-hull-rest, inverted-rest, rest-at-every-attitude, wheels-carry-on-slopes, and `ramp_launch_does_not_explode`. **Known limitation (WI 636):** point-sampled contact can't fully resolve a *sharp vertical cliff* (the test ramp lip); a mid approach-speed crest still clips / small-tosses (no explosion) — the edge-aware heightfield-manifold fix is deferred.

- `/crates/app/` depends on `/crates/sim/` + the `bevy` umbrella + `tiny_http` + `serde_json`.
- `/crates/companion/` depends on `/crates/sim/` (shared `Command`/`Telemetry` types) + `ureq` + `serde_json` + `glam`. No Bevy.

- `Command` and `Telemetry` (`/crates/sim/`) are the shared contract every other crate and the bus depend on — treat changes to them as public-contract changes. The `persist` format (`SavedDocument` + `FORMAT_VERSION`) is a second, **durable** versioned contract: it has its own `FORMAT_VERSION` line (independent of the crate version) and must stay backward-loadable via the two-stage `from_json` migration seam. ## Linear Data Flow

Command lifecycle (the one to know):
1. A source emits a `Command` — keyboard (`/crates/app/src/main.rs`), the companion via `POST /command` (`/crates/app/src/bus.rs` → `drain_commands`), or a future MCP/tablet client.
2. The `Command` becomes a Bevy message.
3. `apply_command` (`/crates/sim/src/command.rs`, in `Update`) drains messages and applies them to `SimClock`/`Craft` — the sole writer of simulation state. Maneuvers apply at the current `clock.time` (impulsive, position-continuous).
4. `OrbitPlugin::advance_clock` advances simulated time by real-frame-time × warp.
5. `publish_telemetry` (`/crates/app/src/bus.rs`) reads the craft's `Orbit` at `clock.time` and writes a `Telemetry` JSON snapshot to a shared buffer each frame; when an active scene has published an `ActiveFlight` block it is attached (WI 569 — the live control tier, not just the orbit); the `tiny_http` thread serves it on `GET /telemetry`. (The on-rails orbit now runs headless behind the 3D scene; it is not the rendered craft.)

Craft derivation (Toy 5): a `VoxelCraft` (in `EditorState`, or loaded from a `SavedDocument`) is the single input; `mass_properties` and `area_curve` (`sim/voxel.rs`) derive mass/CoM/inertia and the aero cross-section from the same voxels. The editor draws them as gizmos each frame; nothing is precomputed or duplicated.

Render placement (Toy 4, retained): entities carry an f64 `WorldPlacement`; `track_anchor` sets the `FloatingOrigin` to the camera's X/Z and `rebase` derives f32 `Transform.translation` as `world − anchor`, pinning the camera to the render origin in X/Z with altitude in Y (the atmosphere consumes that directly).

Wheel contact (Toy 6): `Rover::step` queries the **analytic** `Terrain` (f64) per wheel — never the mesh — computes suspension + tyre + drag forces into a net wrench, and integrates the `ActiveBody` via `integrate_wrench`, sub-stepped at `SUBSTEP_DT`. The rover scene anchors floating-origin rendering at the rover (it sits at a large f64 world offset) and sub-steps the rover each frame.

Resource catch-up (Toy 7): a `ResourceGraph` (`sim/resource.rs`) advances by `integrate(target)` — analytic event-stepping, not tick-by-tick. Each step resolves effective per-reservoir/node net rates under the boundary constraints (empty reservoir's outflow ≤ inflow; full reservoir's inflow ≤ outflow; depleted node draws nothing), then jumps `dt` to the earliest saturation/depletion breakpoint and advances all quantities linearly. The graph holds its own last-integration `time`; nothing drives it from `SimClock` yet (embedding it in the world-save is deferred past the WI 508 hand-off).

Gear hand-off (Toy 8): a `Command::SetGear(kind)` flows through the bus/executor like any command, but is applied by `handoff::apply_gear_switch` (not `apply_command`): it reads the craft's current gear (`Craft` xor `ActiveBody`) plus its persistent `GearState`, builds the other gear's state at `clock.time` (`wake` evaluates the orbit's (r,v); `sleep` fits a conic through the active (r,v)), records the injected `HandoffDiscontinuity` into `LastHandoff`, and swaps the component. Because `wake`/`sleep` are mutual inverses over the shared (r,v) state, the swap injects ~0 jump; the `sim/handoff_discontinuity` diagnostic alarms if it ever does not.

The dive (Toy 9): `descent_step` (`sim/medium.rs`) advances an `ActiveBody` under gravity + drag + buoyancy each sub-step. It samples the one `FluidMedium` at the craft's altitude (`r − surface_radius`); `drag_force` and `buoyancy_force` read only that sample's density (+ submerged voxel volume), so vacuum/atmosphere/ocean are the same code path with different numbers — the multi-fluid thesis. Forces feed `integrate_wrench` (the rover's dissipative integrator). `DiveTriggerPlugin` auto-emits `Command::SetGear(Active)` (and drops warp to 1) when an on-rails craft drops below the entry interface. Since WI 527 **everything is SI** (`CentralBody::EARTHLIKE`): the dive scene runs one craft through orbit→handoff→`DescentPlugin` descent in the same units as the orbit gear — no per-scene unit convention.

Breakage (WI 518): `fracture` (`sim/breakage.rs`) takes a craft + its `ActiveBody` + an applied acceleration, converts the load to the craft's local frame, and runs the stress proxy (`failing_cut`) — axis-aligned cut planes where the outboard inertial load exceeds the crossing-bond strength. The weakest failing cut's bonds are severed and `connected_components` re-partitions the lattice; `split_active` then gives each fragment a momentum-conserving `ActiveBody` (`v = v_cm + ω×r`, inherited spin, re-derived inertia). One structural-change event, not per frame.

Compartments (WI 519): `compartments(craft)` (`sim/compartments.rs`) flood-fills the exterior from the expanded bounding-box border through air cells (solid voxels and closed doors block; open doors pass); the unreached interior air, partitioned into face-connected components, is the set of sealed compartments. A `CompartmentCache` recomputes only when `mark_dirty` is called (build/break/door toggle) — so a scene draws from the cache each frame and recomputes only on an event. Doors are air-topology only (they do not touch mass/aero/breakage).

Flooding (WI 520): a breached `FloodCompartment` (`sim/flooding.rs`) `step`s each active tick against the ambient `FluidSample` at the craft's depth — one ΔP-driven transient: vacuum/atmosphere → air equalises (decompression), liquid → water floods (or implodes past crush strength). `flooded_mass_properties` recomputes the craft's mass + CoM with floodwater point-masses each tick, and the scene feeds that to the `ActiveBody` (heavier → sinks; CoM offset → tilts). Real-time, bounded to active physics — it forces a drop out of warp.

Aero (WI 521): `aero.rs` derives lift, the stability moment, and transonic wave drag from the *same* voxel `area_curve` + `FluidSample` the dive uses for drag. Lift is thin-airfoil (`Cl = 2π·α`, stall) ⊥ to the flow; wave drag exists only where `sound_speed` is defined (a gas), so it is exactly zero in water/vacuum — the medium parameterises the one module. Forces apply to the active craft via `integrate_wrench`, as drag/buoyancy do in the dive.

Gliding re-entry (WI 526): `medium::glide_step` *applies* the aero forces in flight — lift + transonic wave drag added to the dive's central force, plus a lift-at-CoP restoring moment and `aero::pitch_damping_moment` as torque. A statically-stable craft (CoP aft of CoM, via a dense-nose/tapered area curve) entered at a nonzero angle of attack deflects into a glide and weathervanes to trim without tumbling; every term vanishes appropriately in vacuum/liquid (no medium-identity branch). `descent_step` stays as the ballistic reference. The glide is *transient* (a passive craft trims into its deflected path); a *sustained* trimmed cruise needs control (the autopilot thread).

## Known Gaps (not yet built)

- Both gearbox gears exist (on-rails analytic `Orbit`, active numerical `ActiveBody`, WI 515) **and the hand-off between them is now built** (WI 508, `handoff.rs`): a `Command::SetGear` swaps a craft between gears (component swap) at the clock time, validated clean by the hand-off-discontinuity metric. Still open: the switch is **explicitly commanded** — automatic triggers (atmospheric entry, imminent collision, thrust auto-drop) are the dive (Toy 9) and later; the bridge is **planar** (orbital plane = z=0; inclined/3D orbits are an `Orbit`-representation upgrade). **Multi-body SOI is now built** (WI 528, `universe.rs`): `Orbit` is elliptical+hyperbolic, `transform_to` does real inertial-frame transforms, and an on-rails craft re-bases its conic across an SOI boundary (incl. a hyperbolic flyby), continuous in the root frame — though the **active** gear remains single-attractor, and the multi-body universe is a headless capability not yet wired into the running scenes. Units are **SI throughout** (WI 527, `CentralBody::EARTHLIKE`); the automatic atmospheric-entry trigger + the active-gear descent driver (`DescentPlugin`) are built, so one craft runs orbit→handoff→descent in one schedule.
