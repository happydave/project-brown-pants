## Architectural Boundaries

Required: `/crates/sim/` (`sounding_sim`) depends only on Bevy sub-crates (`bevy_app`, `bevy_ecs`, `bevy_time`, `bevy_diagnostic`, `bevy_log`), never the `bevy` umbrella — so it is rendering-free and runs headless.
Forbidden: `/crates/sim/` pulling in rendering or windowing. Verify: `cargo tree -p sounding_sim` shows no `bevy_render`, `bevy_winit`, or `wgpu`.
Required: every change to simulation state is `Command`-driven through the executor — no input system, autopilot, or bus client mutates state directly; they emit `Command` messages. Two arms apply them: **field mutations** (warp, pause, maneuver) go through the pure `apply_command` in `/crates/sim/src/command.rs`; **structural changes** (component insert/remove, spawn/despawn — e.g. the gear swap on `Command::SetGear`, WI 508) go through a dedicated `Command`-triggered system, since they are outside `apply_command`'s `(clock, orbit)` reach. The invariant is the routing (a `Command` drives it), not the single function.
Required: external processes (`/crates/companion/`, future second screen / MCP AI) act on the simulation only via the runtime bus (`GET /telemetry`, `POST /command`). The bus injects `Command`s; it never mutates state.
Required: the Bevy Remote Protocol is compiled only under the app's `dev` feature, absent from default/release builds, and is distinct from the runtime bus.

## Component Index

- **sounding_sim** `/crates/sim/` — the headless simulation core (library).
  - `/crates/sim/src/orbit.rs` — `Orbit`: analytic 2D Kepler propagation (the **on-rails** gear), **elliptical and hyperbolic** (WI 528). `from_state` branches on orbital energy (a>0 bound / a<0 hyperbolic; the parabolic knife-edge is rejected); `position_velocity` propagates both (elliptical `M=E−e·sinE`, hyperbolic `M=e·sinh F−F` via `solve_kepler_hyperbolic`); `is_bound`/`period`/`apoapsis_radius` guard the hyperbolic case. Inputs: μ, state vectors, time. Outputs: position/velocity, derived orbit, maneuver result.
  - `/crates/sim/src/active.rs` — the **active** gear (WI 515): `ActiveBody` numerically integrated under point-mass gravity (velocity Verlet, symplectic) at a fixed `dt`, with torque-free rotation driven by stored world-frame angular momentum (consumes the WI 505 inertia tensor). `advance` caps sub-steps per frame (the active-vehicle warp cap); `ActivePlugin`/`Gravity` drive it from `SimClock`. `integrate_wrench` (semi-implicit Euler under an external force + torque) is the integrator for dissipative/state-dependent actuation — its first consumer is the rover (WI 506). Pure energy/angular-momentum drift functions (WI 499 style). Standalone — the on-rails↔active hand-off is WI 508. Headless.
  - `/crates/sim/src/aero.rs` — **FAR-style aerodynamics: lift, stability, transonic area-ruling** (WI 521), completing the cross-section aero (the dive takes only drag). `lift_coefficient` is thin-airfoil (`2π·α` slope + stall); `lift_force` is `½ρv²·Cl·A` ⊥ to the flow (any dense fluid, zero in vacuum). `sound_speed` = `√(γP/ρ)` for a **gas only** (`None` for liquid/vacuum — water is incompressible by design), gating `mach` and the transonic `wave_drag_coefficient` (a bump near Mach 1, scaled by the `area_ruling_factor` of the area curve — smooth bodies get less). `center_of_pressure`/`pitching_moment` give the restoring (weathervane) moment, and `pitch_damping_moment` (WI 526) is the `Cm_q`-style aerodynamic damping torque that makes a statically-stable craft *converge* to trim rather than oscillate (zero in vacuum/at rest). All from the one `area_curve` + one `FluidSample`; headless, unit-tested (incl. the 2π lift slope, the spike vanishing in water/vacuum, and damping opposing spin). **Applied in flight by the dive's `glide_step` (WI 526)** — no longer coefficients-only.

Headless, unit-tested (continuous descent reaches the ocean bounded; one-path drag/buoyancy; glide-vs-ballistic deflection; trim-without-tumble; medium-gated wave drag; **the full SI chain orbit→handoff→descent→ocean in one schedule**).

Headless, unit-tested.
  - `/crates/sim/src/contact.rs` — **contact response** (WI 592, WI 593): a penalty resolver (spring-damper normal + regularized Coulomb friction) over the **relative** contact-point velocity, sharing one `penalty_contact_force` helper. `ground_contact_wrench` (craft↔static-ground) and `body_contact_wrench` (**craft↔craft, equal-and-opposite**, WI 593) both return force+torque about each CoM (lattice shape placed at `dry_com`), applied via `ActiveBody::integrate_wrench` — no new mutation path. The damping force is **clamped to the spring force** so the first-contact "damping kick" can't inject energy (the body↔body stability fix; a no-op for resting/landing, so WI 592/597 ground behavior is preserved). Bounciness is damping-governed (no per-material restitution; WI 593 decision). Unit-tested: craft settling to rest, head-on pair (no energy injection), a stable two-craft stack, determinism.
  - `/crates/sim/src/breakage.rs` — **connected-component structural breakage** (WI 518), the voxel model's third role. `connected_components` partitions a (severable) lattice into maximal face-connected fragment crafts. `failing_cut`/`break_craft` are the **stress proxy, not FEA**: a candidate axis-aligned cut fails when the inertial load across it (`|Σ_outboard m·a|`) exceeds the crossing bonds' strength (count × cell-face-area × material tensile strength) — one cross-section comparison, no field/solve. `split_active`/`fracture` are the **momentum-conserving** rigid split: each fragment inherits the parent's linear + angular velocity (`v = v_cm + ω×r`), re-deriving its own mass/inertia, conserving total momentum. `fracture_on_impact` (WI 594) is the **collision→breakage coupling**: a contact force `F` (from `contact.rs`) transmitted through the structure decelerates the CoM at `F/m`, which is exactly the applied acceleration the stress proxy consumes — so a hard impact fractures and a gentle touchdown doesn't, against the same per-material strength threshold. Headless, unit-tested.
  - `/crates/sim/src/warp.rs` — **anti-tunnelling time-warp cap** (WI 595): pure, stateless policy. `max_safe_warp` (and its substep-size twin `safe_substep_dt`) returns the largest warp for which one warp-scaled active step closes a body's gap to a surface by no more than `gap + tolerance`, so the fixed-step integrator can't pass through before the contact stage runs; `ground_safe_warp` applies it to a half-space using `collision::craft_bounding_radius` (orientation-invariant CoM sphere). Receding/far → warp unchanged. No stored state — applied as a `min` where warp is resolved, consistent with the `SimClock` warp model. **CCD/swept tests are deferred** (recorded decision): the proximity cap covers the project's warp range; the live active gear also stays count-capped at `MAX_SUBSTEPS`. Headless, unit-tested (cap prevents tunnelling where uncapped warp tunnels straight through).
  - `/crates/sim/src/compartments.rs` — **airtight compartments** (WI 519), the voxel model's fourth role. `compartments(craft)` derives the sealed interior volumes by flood-fill: the solid set (voxels ∪ closed doors) bounds an exterior flood from the one-cell-expanded bbox border through air cells (open doors are passable); the empty cells the exterior cannot reach are partitioned into face-connected components (`Compartment { cells, volume }`, `CompartmentSet`). A solid block has none, a hollow shell one, an internal wall two, an open door merges, a hull breach vents. `CompartmentCache` recomputes **only** when `mark_dirty` (build/break/door toggle), never per frame. Headless, unit-tested.
  - `/crates/sim/src/flooding.rs` — **decompression / flooding / implosion** (WI 520): the design's sign-flipped pressure-differential transient, real-time (active-gear, not the WI 507 analytic graph). A `FloodCompartment` (volume, centroid, floodwater, trapped-air `air_pv`, breached, crush strength) `step`s against a `FluidSample`: `ΔP = P_ambient − P_internal` drives **one** model — gas ambient equalises the air toward ambient (decompression when the cabin is higher), liquid ambient with `ΔP>0` floods water toward full (air venting), `ΔP` past crush strength implodes (catastrophic flood). `flooded_mass_properties` folds floodwater point-masses into the dry mass/CoM — the one place a resource feeds back into rigid-body dynamics in real time. Headless, unit-tested; no durable-format change (runtime state).
  - `/crates/sim/src/resource.rs` — the **resource/converter system** (Toy 7, WI 507): one data-driven `ResourceGraph` of `Reservoir`s, depletable `ResourceNode`s (mining targets), `Converter`s (recipes; an extractor is a converter drawing a node), `Consumer`s, and `Conduit`s (inter-reservoir flow), with the **analytic on-rails catch-up integrator** `integrate(target)`. Flows are piecewise-constant, so the integrator event-steps to each reservoir-saturation / node-depletion breakpoint in closed form — cost bounded by saturation events, not by the warp interval (the warp filter). Boundary-relaxation throttling keeps every reservoir in `[0, capacity]` and every node `≥ 0`; conservation is recipe-defined. Standalone and headless (the graph carries its own last-integration time); persist embedding is the hand-off seam (WI 508). Serde-derivable, unit-tested (incl. catch-up ≡ tick-by-tick).
  - `/crates/sim/src/propulsion.rs` — **engine thrust + propellant** (devices-as-active, WI 531; the first-playable initiative): an `Engine` (device profile — propellant tank, exhaust velocity, max mass-flow, mount, axis, gimbal) draws propellant from a `ResourceGraph` reservoir at a throttle-scaled rate and emits a **thrust wrench** (force along the gimballed axis at the mount → force + moment about the CoM) via `ActiveBody::integrate_wrench` — the same path drag/lift use. `Propulsion::thrust_step` rations shared tanks, deducts propellant, and flames out on empty; `wet_mass` folds tank propellant into the dry mass/CoM in real time (the `flooding` pattern, so the rocket equation emerges — Tsiolkovsky-validated); `delta_v` is the rocket equation `v_e·ln(m_wet/m_dry)` (WI 535, the HUD readout); `apply_command` maps `Command::SetThrottle`/`SetGimbal` to engine state. Active-gear, real-time (thrust forces a drop out of warp); on-rails analytic burns deferred. A headless capability — the ECS system sub-stepping it into a scene is the game-session/control items. Serde-derivable, unit-tested.
  - `/crates/sim/src/launch.rs` — **launch-pad rest + release** (surface launch, WI 532): `LaunchPad` models the ground as a **unilateral vertical support** — *not* a stiff contact (no kraken). While net force along local up ≤ 0 the craft is held at **exact rest** (zero velocity, clamped to the pad, not integrated → no jitter/penetration); the instant thrust overcomes weight (net-up > 0) it **releases** into `integrate_wrench`. Surfaces a launch-stability diagnostic (lift-off acceleration, max penetration). The caller assembles the net wrench (gravity + thrust + drag); the pad only decides hold/release/integrate. Headless, unit-tested (incl. the full WI 531 engine-throttle-up → lift-off chain).
  - `/crates/sim/src/attitude.rs` — **attitude control** (RCS, reaction wheels, SAS; WI 533): `ReactionWheels` (per-axis torque, **saturating** at a stored-momentum limit — storing the opposite momentum, so total is conserved), `Rcs` (an external jet drawing propellant from a `ResourceGraph` reservoir, flame-out), `AttitudeControl::actuate` (wheels first, then RCS). `Sas` is the PD control law (modes `Off`/`KillRotation`/`Hold`/`Point`) producing a body-frame torque from orientation/rate error; `AttitudePilot` bundles SAS + manual intent + actuators and `step`s the achieved torque through `integrate_wrench`. The `persist` format (`SavedDocument` + `FORMAT_VERSION`) is a second, **durable** versioned contract: it has its own `FORMAT_VERSION` line (independent of the crate version) and must stay backward-loadable via the two-stage `from_json` migration seam. ## Linear Data Flow

Command lifecycle (the one to know):
1. A source emits a `Command` — keyboard (`/crates/app/src/main.rs`), the companion via `POST /command` (`/crates/app/src/bus.rs` → `drain_commands`), or a future MCP/tablet client.
2. The `Command` becomes a Bevy message.
3. `apply_command` (`/crates/sim/src/command.rs`, in `Update`) drains messages and applies them to `SimClock`/`Craft` — the sole writer of simulation state. Maneuvers apply at the current `clock.time` (impulsive, position-continuous).
4. `OrbitPlugin::advance_clock` advances simulated time by real-frame-time × warp.
5. `publish_telemetry` (`/crates/app/src/bus.rs`) reads the craft's `Orbit` at `clock.time` and writes a `Telemetry` JSON snapshot to a shared buffer each frame; when an active scene has published an `ActiveFlight` block it is attached (WI 569 — the live control tier, not just the orbit); the `tiny_http` thread serves it on `GET /telemetry`. (The on-rails orbit now runs headless behind the 3D scene; it is not the rendered craft.)

Craft derivation (Toy 5): a `VoxelCraft` (in `EditorState`, or loaded from a `SavedDocument`) is the single input; `mass_properties` and `area_curve` (`sim/voxel.rs`) derive mass/CoM/inertia and the aero cross-section from the same voxels. The editor draws them as gizmos each frame; nothing is precomputed or duplicated.

Render placement (Toy 4, retained): entities carry an f64 `WorldPlacement`; `track_anchor` sets the `FloatingOrigin` to the camera's X/Z and `rebase` derives f32 `Transform.translation` as `world − anchor`, pinning the camera to the render origin in X/Z with altitude in Y (the atmosphere consumes that directly).

Wheel contact (Toy 6): `Rover::step` queries the **analytic** `Terrain` (f64) per wheel — never the mesh — computes suspension + tyre + drag forces into a net wrench, and integrates the `ActiveBody` via `integrate_wrench`, sub-stepped at `SUBSTEP_DT`. The rover scene anchors floating-origin rendering at the rover (it sits at a large f64 world offset) and sub-steps the rover each frame.

Resource catch-up (Toy 7): a `ResourceGraph` (`sim/resource.rs`) advances by `integrate(target)` — analytic event-stepping, not tick-by-tick. Each step resolves effective per-reservoir/node net rates under the boundary constraints (empty reservoir's outflow ≤ inflow; full reservoir's inflow ≤ outflow; depleted node draws nothing), then jumps `dt` to the earliest saturation/depletion breakpoint and advances all quantities linearly. The graph holds its own last-integration `time`; nothing drives it from `SimClock` yet (embedding it in the world-save is deferred past the WI 508 hand-off).

Gear hand-off (Toy 8): a `Command::SetGear(kind)` flows through the bus/executor like any command, but is applied by `handoff::apply_gear_switch` (not `apply_command`): it reads the craft's current gear (`Craft` xor `ActiveBody`) plus its persistent `GearState`, builds the other gear's state at `clock.time` (`wake` evaluates the orbit's (r,v); `sleep` fits a conic through the active (r,v)), records the injected `HandoffDiscontinuity` into `LastHandoff`, and swaps the component. Because `wake`/`sleep` are mutual inverses over the shared (r,v) state, the swap injects ~0 jump; the `sim/handoff_discontinuity` diagnostic alarms if it ever does not.

The dive (Toy 9): `descent_step` (`sim/medium.rs`) advances an `ActiveBody` under gravity + drag + buoyancy each sub-step. It samples the one `FluidMedium` at the craft's altitude (`r − surface_radius`); `drag_force` and `buoyancy_force` read only that sample's density (+ submerged voxel volume), so vacuum/atmosphere/ocean are the same code path with different numbers — the multi-fluid thesis. Forces feed `integrate_wrench` (the rover's dissipative integrator). `DiveTriggerPlugin` auto-emits `Command::SetGear(Active)` (and drops warp to 1) when an on-rails craft drops below the entry interface. Since WI 527 **everything is SI** (`CentralBody::EARTHLIKE`): the dive scene runs one craft through orbit→handoff→`DescentPlugin` descent in the same units as the orbit gear — no per-scene unit convention.

Breakage (WI 518): `fracture` (`sim/breakage.rs`) takes a craft + its `ActiveBody` + an applied acceleration, converts the load to the craft's local frame, and runs the stress proxy (`failing_cut`) — axis-aligned cut planes where the outboard inertial load exceeds the crossing-bond strength. The weakest failing cut's bonds are severed and `connected_components` re-partitions the lattice; `split_active` then gives each fragment a momentum-conserving `ActiveBody` (`v = v_cm + ω×r`, inherited spin, re-derived inertia). One structural-change event, not per frame.

Compartments (WI 519): `compartments(craft)` (`sim/compartments.rs`) flood-fills the exterior from the expanded bounding-box border through air cells (solid voxels and closed doors block; open doors pass); the unreached interior air, partitioned into face-connected components, is the set of sealed compartments. A `CompartmentCache` recomputes only when `mark_dirty` is called (build/break/door toggle) — so a scene draws from the cache each frame and recomputes only on an event. Doors are air-topology only (they do not touch mass/aero/breakage).

Flooding (WI 520): a breached `FloodCompartment` (`sim/flooding.rs`) `step`s each active tick against the ambient `FluidSample` at the craft's depth — one ΔP-driven transient: vacuum/atmosphere → air equalises (decompression), liquid → water floods (or implodes past crush strength). `flooded_mass_properties` recomputes the craft's mass + CoM with floodwater point-masses each tick, and the scene feeds that to the `ActiveBody` (heavier → sinks; CoM offset → tilts). Real-time, bounded to active physics — it forces a drop out of warp.

Aero (WI 521): `aero.rs` derives lift, the stability moment, and transonic wave drag from the *same* voxel `area_curve` + `FluidSample` the dive uses for drag. Lift is thin-airfoil (`Cl = 2π·α`, stall) ⊥ to the flow; wave drag exists only where `sound_speed` is defined (a gas), so it is exactly zero in water/vacuum — the medium parameterises the one module. Forces apply to the active craft via `integrate_wrench`, as drag/buoyancy do in the dive.

Gliding re-entry (WI 526): `medium::glide_step` *applies* the aero forces in flight — lift + transonic wave drag added to the dive's central force, plus a lift-at-CoP restoring moment and `aero::pitch_damping_moment` as torque. A statically-stable craft (CoP aft of CoM, via a dense-nose/tapered area curve) entered at a nonzero angle of attack deflects into a glide and weathervanes to trim without tumbling; every term vanishes appropriately in vacuum/liquid (no medium-identity branch). `descent_step` stays as the ballistic reference. The glide is *transient* (a passive craft trims into its deflected path); a *sustained* trimmed cruise needs control (the autopilot thread).
